Interviews

For our primary research, our team visited and interviewed 3 remarkable individuals, that can directly reflect the current situation of Dong cuisine in the modern world. Below are the Interview results

Primary Research: Interview with Restaurant Owner

Question: How do you decide which dishes to include on the menu to ensure customers will like them?

Owner's Response: We follow traditional Dong cuisine, such as cured meat. For special occasions, we serve premium dishes like fish and meat to our guests.

Question: Have you ever changed the menu based on customer preferences?

Owner's Response: Yes, we try to show our hospitality by offering fresh dishes. We also include some popular dishes like stir-fries to cater to broader tastes, but most of our menu features our unique specialties.

Question: When purchasing ingredients, do you notice that different generations prefer different foods?

Owner's Response: The purchasing methods have changed over time. Nowadays, we use platforms like Meituan to buy ingredients online, which gives us more variety and less limitation.

Question: Have you recently introduced any new dishes that are not part of traditional Dong cuisine but are popular with customers?

Owner's Response: We haven't introduced many completely new dishes, but we do modify our menu based on customer feedback to better suit their tastes.

Primary Research: Interview with Local Resident

Question: Over the years, how have traditional Dong cooking customs changed?

Response: There have been significant changes. Previously, we used firewood and clay stoves; now we use gas stoves.

Question: Could you share some family-inherited foods?

Response: None, I'm not sure.

Question: What is your favorite traditional Dong food?

Response: Sour fish soup and sour pork.

Question: Have external influences changed what you plant and how you farm?

Response: Previously, harvesting rice involved elderly using sickles and cotton cloth for collection. Now, we use machinery.

Primary Research: Interview with Local Elder

Question: How have traditional Dong cooking methods changed over the years?

Response: In the past, we used firewood and clay stoves made from mud. Now, electricity makes things more convenient.

Question: Have any family recipes been passed down from generation to generation that are now at risk of being lost?

Response: Beef tripe soup and cured meat are specialties passed down from childhood. Previous dishes were homemade, with ingredients gathered and picked ourselves.

Question: What traditional methods are still used today?

Response: We still grow and cook our own vegetables, including fresh wild mountain vegetables. We also raise our own livestock.

Question: Have external influences changed what you grow?

Response: Yes, we now refer to local agricultural practices and grow vegetables based on customer preferences and cost-effectiveness.
